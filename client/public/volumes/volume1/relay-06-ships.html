RELAY 6: SHIPS

The Maritime Revolution - Global Reach

Mastering the Seas

Ships enabled humanity to transcend land-based limitations and achieve global reach. Maritime infrastructure—ports, shipyards, navigation systems, supply networks—transformed civilizations that mastered them into global powers. The Age of Exploration was driven by ship infrastructure.

Ship infrastructure included not just vessels, but also ports, shipyards, navigation knowledge, supply systems, and naval organizations. Maritime civilizations—from Phoenicia to Venice to Portugal to Britain—dominated their eras through superior ship infrastructure.

Control of sea lanes meant control of global trade. Naval power became the ultimate expression of civilizational might. The rise and fall of empires could be traced through their maritime infrastructure.

HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE

Timeline: Approximately 2,000 years ago through the Age of Exploration (15th-18th centuries).

Impact: Enabled global trade networks, facilitated cultural exchange, and enabled European colonial expansion. Maritime civilizations became the dominant powers of the modern era.

Legacy: Modern shipping infrastructure remains critical to global commerce. Container ships, bulk carriers, and tankers are the direct descendants of ancient maritime infrastructure.
